# Someone else's sandbox

*A stone for an individual. 2026-06-23.*

**The read.** The cornerstone asks whether a thing earns its place or you're
just used to it. This goes one layer under that: the box itself — the sandbox,
and the god it's for — was never ours to own. We didn't pour the frame and we
don't keep it. In the game you don't own the law either; a thing moves when
unwatched whether you consent or not. So what's left to hold isn't the box. It's
the stance inside it. Three are offered — play, study, do — and a single word
governs all three: *responsibly*. The crappy ninja is the proof of concept:
sincere, visible, bad at it, and fine — because he isn't performing for a god
who's his. He's just doing the thing, in the open, in a box he doesn't own.

**Open question for an individual.** In a box that was never yours — do you
play, study, or do? Name your one, and say what makes it *responsible*.
