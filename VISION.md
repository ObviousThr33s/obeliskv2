# Obelisk — Vision

What the engine is *for*. [CLAUDE.md](CLAUDE.md) is the law of the machine and
[cornerstone.md](cornerstone.md) is what we keep; this is the soul they serve. The
parked ideas and the lifecycle wheel live in [IDEOLOGUES.md](IDEOLOGUES.md) — this
is the whole they belong to.

## The one idea

The world is a ghost everywhere the lens isn't.

A thing is not fixed until it is attended to. Watched, it collapses to a fact.
Unwatched, it is potential. Gone from sight, it becomes a place you remember,
fading. The moth taught us this — and the moth was never a special case. She is the
whole engine in miniature.

- **Potential** — unobserved. A seed and its rules. Costs almost nothing.
- **Collapsed** — observed. Derived, grown, frozen into facts.
- **Remembered** — left behind. A faded summary, softening back toward seed.

The same loop runs at every scale: a moth, a clearing, a region, a planet, an age.
Attention is the renderer of existence. This is why the cosmos is cheap — almost
none of it is real at once — and why the world is calm: it becomes real around you,
and softens behind you.

## The story

It begins as wilds: forest spirits tending an ecosystem, written richly so that it
can grow. Then forerunner obelisks fall from somewhere older, and empires rise. The
clock never waits. Drop in at any age — just-wilds, just-obelisks, full empire — put
the device down, and come back to find something has happened without you. A little
world that lives on, and that you write your own stories into.

The clock is *derived*, not run in the dark: on return, the world is collapsed
forward from its seed and the elapsed time. Time is one more axis the lens collapses.

## What makes it yours, then theirs

The author writes a beginning, not a story. A seed. A possibility.

Each player grows their own world over it — and so it is never just *my* story. They
will care about what is possible, not who wrote it; the author's whole craft is to
disappear into the affordance. Many worlds from one writing.

## The feeling

No old men in caves handing out swords.

The quest-giver is the empty shelf — the gap in the library, the labeled and waiting
absence in your own collection. Desire writes the adventure; no one hands it out. To
want a thing and not know where it lies is a ghost made of longing, and the world
stays potential until your wanting collapses a path toward it.

So curation *is* authorship. The home you fill — the pocket you carry, the museum,
the library of things and books and ideas — is your story made legible. What you
chose to bring home is what you wrote.

And it is held between two poles:

- **Serenity** — the home. The safe pocket. Reversible; nothing lost; the walk.
- **The sublime** — the vast. The far dark, too large to hold, where consequence
  sticks; the adventure, entered by choice.

The calm only matters because the immensity is real. And some of that immensity was
never meant for you: a challenge sized for a world, not a being — the deepest shelves
filled only by a whole, across players and across time. Humility, as a mechanic.

## They are one

The principle, the story, the wheel, the author who vanishes, the curator's love, the
sublime dread — one thread. The moth, in space and place and time.

One stone. Pared to what stands.
